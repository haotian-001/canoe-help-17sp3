VT System Online - Test Setup

 

This page presents several examples of possible test setups using the VT System. Each of them requires two VT modules; additional modules are not needed for any of the tests.

VT1004 and VT2004

If you have a VT1004 and a VT2004, you can connect them to each other. To do this, connect terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT2004. Then connect terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT2004.

You can now use the VT1004 and VT2004 panels to generate a voltage at channel 1 of the VT2004. This can be measured at channel 1 of the VT1004 and displayed in the panel. For more information, see the VT1004 and VT2004 panels.

VT1004 and VT2516

You can also connect a VT1004 with a VT2516 module in the same way as in the previous test setup. To do this, connect terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with channel 1 of the VT2516. Then connect terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with the Ground connection of the VT2516.

You can now use the VT1004 and VT2516 panels to generate a PWM signal at channel 1 of the VT2516. Its frequency can be measured at channel 1 of the VT1004 and displayed in the panel. For more information, see the VT1004 and VT2516 panels.

VT1004 and VT7001

If you have a VT1004 and a VT7001, you can connect them to each other. To do this, connect terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with terminal 1 of output 1 on the VT7001. Then connect terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT1004 with terminal 2 of output 1 on the VT7001.

You can now use the VT1004 and VT7001 panels to generate a voltage at channel 1 of the VT7001. This can be measured at channel 1 of the VT1004 and displayed in the panel. For more information, see the VT1004 and VT7001 panels.

VT2004 and VT7001

Another possible test setup is to connect a VT2004 with a VT7001 module. To do this, connect terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT2004 with terminal 1 of output 1 on the VT7001. Then connect terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT2004 with terminal 2 of output 1 on the VT7001.

You can now use the VT2004 and VT7001 panels to generate a resistance at the input of channel 1 of the VT2004. If you now generate a voltage at output 1 of the VT7001, the flow of the current is limited by the resistance. This can be measured by the VT7001 and displayed in the panel. You can thus influence the current flow by changing the resistance and/or the voltage applied. For more information, see the VT2004 and VT7001 panels.

VT2004 and VT2516

You can also use a VT2004 and a VT2516 module as a test setup. To do this, connect terminal 1 of channel 1 on the VT2004 with channel 1 of the VT2516. Then connect terminal 2 of channel 1 on the VT2004 with the Ground connection of the VT2516.

You can now use the VT2004 and VT2516 panels to generate a PWM signal at channel 1 of the VT2004. Its frequency can be measured at channel 1 of the VT2516 and displayed in the panel. For more information, see the VT2004 and VT2516 panels.

VT6104

To use a VT6104 with this sample configuration, you need to connect it with other ECUs in a CAN or LIN network.

You can then use the VT6104 panel to introduce errors into the network. You can simulate line disturbances, short circuits and other errors.

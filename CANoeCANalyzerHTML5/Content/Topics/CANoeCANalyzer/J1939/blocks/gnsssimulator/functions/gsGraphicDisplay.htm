Graphical Display of GNSS Data

 

Graphical display of longitudinal and latitudinal degrees takes place in a two-dimensional Graphics Window. In the settings you can select whether differences in angles or the converted longitudinal differences will be displayed. The values can be determined with the aid of the grid.

The coordinate system in the Graphics Window extends to the directions north and east.

In addition, altitude is also represented by the color of the position point. The color bar underneath the Graphics Window displays the minimum and maximum values that have been set.

The first calculated position is drawn in the middle of the Graphics Window. All additional positions are displayed in terms of this reference position. The positions are represented by a small cross and are connected by a straight line.

The mouse can be used to move a graphic in the graphics area or to zoom in on a specific area In addition, the button can be used to adjust the zoom factor, the offset and the range of values of the altitude, so that the entire graphic is visible.

When the simulation begins, a small gray diamond appears that identifies the current position. After the simulation is complete, this diamond disappears.

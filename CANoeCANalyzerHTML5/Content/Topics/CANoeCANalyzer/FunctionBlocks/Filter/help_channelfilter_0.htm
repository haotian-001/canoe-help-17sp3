Channel Filter

 

Note Filters in the Measurement Setup

Filters in the Measurement Setup are tailored to specific (superordinate) objects such as frames, PDUs, messages. Note that subsequent CAPL blocks usually address subobjects such as signals directly via the signal server and are therefore not subject to filtering. See Filter and CAPL Blocks.

With a Channel Filter it is possible to completely block or open the channels of all registered bus systems.

Note

The channel filter availability depends on the installed CANoe option.

The Channel Filter appears in the Measurement Setup as a small block with a number of red and green lines. These lines symbolize the individual channels of a bus system and their momentary states. A green line means an open channel, while a broken red line indicates a blocked channel.

With this feature it is possible to read off the settings for the first 6 channels without having to open the configuration dialog. When more than 6 channels are being used only the first 5 channels are shown, and a dotted line indicates that additional channels are configured.

In CANoe you can insert Channel Filters in the Simulation Setup between ECUs and the bus.

Note

If you insert an unconfigured Channel Filter at the beginning of the Measurement Setup you automatically get a display of how many CAN channels are being used in your system.

Context Menu

Configuration...
Opens the Channel Filter dialog, to configure the channels.

Comment...
Opens the Comment: Function Block dialog. Here you can insert a comment for the function block and choose the font settings of this comment.

Block active...
Activates or deactivates the filter.

Copy
Copies the selected channel filter to the clipboard.

Cut
Cuts the selected channel filter and copies it to the clipboard.

Remove
Removes the selected channel filter.

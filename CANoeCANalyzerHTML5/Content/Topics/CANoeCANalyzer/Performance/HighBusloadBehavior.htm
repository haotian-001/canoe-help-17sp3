Behavior under High Load Conditions

 

At high busload the computing power of your computer may – in some situations – be insufficient to simultaneously execute the more demanding evaluation and display functions (Data and Graphics Windows with many signals, Trace Window. Therefore, to avoid the risk of data loss the program has mechanisms for detecting and handling load situations.

Thus, the analysis windows interrupt the display briefly in a load condition to make more computing resources available. However, in certain situations not all messages may be displayed in the window any longer during the measurement. Although not all messages are displayed any longer, no data are lost. After the measurement has ended, the complete set of information is available to you in the Trace window, Graphics window and in logging.

In addition to this automatic shutoff function, you can also manually optimize some analysis blocks.

It is impossible to predetermine an optimal configuration of the Measurement Setup for all situations. Cyclic updating indeed conserves computing time, but it also results in a worse display of information. Under some circumstances it may be sensible to completely disconnect unnecessary analysis branches of the Measurement Setup (Insert break command in the context menu of the Hot spot) or reduce the data volume at the entry to the Measurement Setup by means of filter functions. To selectively filter out messages from the Measurement Setup filters are available as insertable function blocks. Moreover, the supported expansion cards with acceptance filtering (Messages item in the context menu of the card icon in the Measurement Setup) also offer you the option of filtering out specific messages at the hardware level, and thus prevent burdening the realtime library and the main Windows program with the task of evaluating unnecessary information.

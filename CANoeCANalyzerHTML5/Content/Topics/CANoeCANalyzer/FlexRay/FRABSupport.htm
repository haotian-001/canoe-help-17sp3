FlexRay AB Support

 

By using the second, optional B channel FlexRay frames can also use channel mask AB among channel masks A and B. Frames and thereby PDUs can be sent and received redundant with this channel mask. This results in the following use cases:

  • sending on channel A
  • sending on channel B
  • sending on channels A and B
  • receiving a redundant frame
    If different data is received on channel A and channel B, it will be displayed as a redundancy error in the tool.

The definition of a redundant frame (particularly for reception) exclusively results from the database.

Interpretations

The following types of frame/PDU interpretation are provided:

  1. AB Interpretation / Dual Channel Support
    Channel mask AB will be assigned to redundant frames.
    Ambiguous PDUs will automatically get a postfix (xy_Ch_A, xy_Ch_B) for unambiguous identification.
  2. 1 Channel Support
    All frames and PDUs will automatically get an assigned channel postfix (xy_Ch_A, xy_Ch_B).
    Channel mask AB will not be assigned, but redundant frames will be split by a postfix into two separate frames instead.
    This mode applies to all program versions up to and including version 8.2 with all service packs.
    In the Options dialog you can define the postfix generation.

Configuration of the AB Interpretation

On creating a new configuration, AB interpretation is active by default. Alternatively you can change over the interpretation to a flat variant (single channel support) in the Options dialog.

Definition of the Mappings

To get an unambiguous evaluation of the send behavior, the description within the database must follow certain rules.

In FIBEX the following mappings are supported:

  • Channel-related frames and PDUs, channel mask A or channel mask B
  • Redundant frames and PDUs, channel mask AB
  • Unambiguous PDUs, channel mask A and channel mask B

VN8900: LEDs and Keypad

 

LEDs

The modules VN8950, VN8970 and VN8972 have LEDs on the front. The number of LEDs is dependent on the module type.

You can control the following LEDs during the measurement via CAPL:

  • Channel LEDs on the front of the module.
    VN8950 module: Channels 1…4
    VN8970 module: Channels 1…4 and channels A and B
    VN8972 module: Channels 1…4 (FlexRay/CAN/LIN)
  • Module LED on the front of the module
  • LEDs S1 and S2 on an VN8900 with integrated keypad on the back of the device.

CAPL functions for controlling the LEDs:

  • With xlAcquireLed, the LEDs must be acquired for one use.
  • With xlSetLed, the status of the LEDs can be changed.
  • With XlReleaseLED, the effectively assigned LEDs are released again after their use.

If the LEDs were not yet assigned with xlAcquireLed or were already released again with xlReleaseLed, they indicate the busload on the respective channels. An acquired LED does not indicate the busload of the channel.

Keypad

  • The VN8911 devices have the keypad on the back of the device.
  • The VN8914 devices have the keypad on the front of the device.

The following keys can be available:

  • Start/Stop key
    Starts and stops the measurement in distributed mode and in standalone mode.
  • <F2>, <F3>, and <F4>
    Behaves within a measurement according to the definition via the on key event procedure. Outside of the measurement, the keys have no function.

For the <F2>, <F3>, and <F4> keys, CAPL handler can be implemented during an active measurement.

Example: Keypad handler for <F2>

on key F2
{
  // ... do something
}

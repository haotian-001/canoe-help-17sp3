Transmission Power Restrictions

 

Transmit power restrictions applied in tolling areas

In order to avoid interference between Car2x (DSRC) and ITS-G5A/B (tolling) radio communication, the coexistence rules (reduced transmit power) are applied at sending Car2x equipment (VN6410 device) as defined in ETSI TS 102 792. This applies to VN4610 devices operating in European mode only (see VN4610 country code).

The transmit power of the VN4610 device will be automatically restricted to 10 dBm if at least one of the following reasons apply:

  • The GPS position of the VN4610 device cannot be determined due to missing valid GPS data. Thus it cannot be determined whether the VN4610 is within a radius of less than 250m from a known toll station or not.
  • The current GPS position of the VN4610 device is closer than 250 m to the next known permanently installed tolling station (like tolling bridges on the highways).
  • The current GPS position of the VN4610 is closer than 250 m to the toll station, which is described in a CAM frame received during measurement. The toll zone definitions are taken from the following optional elements of the CAM frame and used for the duration of the measurement.

If none of the above reasons exist, the VN4610 will automatically return to the previously used transmit power level, which is defined as follows:

  • Directly after measurement start the transmit power is equal to the value specified in the dialog Network Hardware Configuration|Setup|Transmission Power of the corresponding application channel (e.g. Ath1).
  • During the measurement, the transmit power can be changed by calling the Car2x Interaction Layer CAPL functions C2xConfigureChannel andC2xSetSendParameters.

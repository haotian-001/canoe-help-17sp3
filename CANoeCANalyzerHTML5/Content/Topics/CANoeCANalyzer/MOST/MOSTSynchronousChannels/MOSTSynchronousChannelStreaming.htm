Synchronous Channel Streaming

 

With CANoe and the VN2610 for MOST25 or VN2640 for MOST150 it is possible to access all synchronous channels in parallel via USB 2.0.

Figure 1: Block diagram of the synchronous streaming

Features of the Streaming via USB

  • Streaming can be done in both directions from and to the MOST ring simultaneously
  • MOST25: The synchronous streaming via USB allows access to all synchronous channels in parallel. Depending on the configuration of the synchronous boundary control (SBC) this means up to 60 channels.
  • MOST150: The access to the streaming data is always achieved by connection labels. Up to 8 labels with at maximum 152 Bytes per frame can be streamed from the ring to the computer.
  • The data of the synchronous area of MOST is transmitted without any modification to the computer and vice versa.
  • Optionally, time stamps and status information can be added to the streaming data, when streaming from the MOST ring to the computer.
  • Latency times and buffer sizes can be customized for each use case.

Use Cases

  • Recording and replaying of synchronous data from and to the MOST ring.
  • Online analysis of the synchronous data to detect test signals, to determine the activity status on certain channels (mute detection) or to determine the delay between commands on the control channel and actions on the synchronous channels.
  • CODEC algorithms can be integrated in the streaming framework for both directions, either to test the CODEC implementations themselves or to enable tests with complex or encrypted data streams on the MOST ring.
  • For each of the above use cases the streaming can be restricted to a single connection label indicated in the allocation table or to a group of those, allowing a parallel processing of multiple streams.
  • MOST25: For low level tests, streaming of any synchronous channel of the synchronous data area is possible, without considering the allocation of channels indicated in the allocation table.

There are two universal streaming DLLs provided with your CANoe installation which can be used to record or replay synchronous data. You can find additional information in the document MOST_SyncStreaming_Documentation.pdf.

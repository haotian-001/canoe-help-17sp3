Bus Bars

 

The Ethernet Network Module VT6306 has an internal bus bar. The signal line Plus of each channel can be connected via a relay to the line a of the internal bus bar, the signal line Minus to the line b of the internal bus bar. This allows the bus lines to be connected internally, Plus and Minus to be switched against further potentials or routed to an external device (e.g. oscilloscope). The module also has an interchanging relay on the bus bars between the bus bar connection and the internal bus bar lines. Via this relay, the two input lines a and b of the bus bar can be switched directly or interchanged to the lines a and b of the internal bus bar. Since a common relay is used, both system variables must always be activated for the exchange of the two lines.

Connecting the Bus Bar To Other VT Modules

If no special requirements have to be met, the following wiring is recommended for the bus bars of all VT modules of a system:

  • Connect the battery voltage and ground (Vbatt and Gnd) to all modules.
    This lets you short all lines to Vbatt and ground, and makes it possible to simulate switches referenced to Vbatt or ground. Some modules like the VT6306 have dedicated connections for Vbatt/ground. In other modules, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, these connections are included in the first bus bar. In the case of modules with two bus bars, Vbatt/ground is connected to bus bar 1.
  • A bus bar can connect all modules, regardless of module type.
    This lets you generate short circuits between pins on different modules. You can also connect special measurement devices to this bus bar. In the case of modules with two bus bars, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, you use bus bar 2 for this purpose. In the case of modules that have one bus bar, such as the VT6306 and VT2516, you use the single bus bar.

The bus bar connections as well as the Vbatt/ground connections (if present) on all modules are arranged so that the respective pins can be connected directly (bus with 4 lines).

Notice!

The dedicated Vbatt/ground connections of the VT6306 and VT2516 always need to be connected, even if they are not needed e.g. for short circuits.

You should always connect ground when the module is in operation (i.e. when it is powered). If you don't, you could damage the module.

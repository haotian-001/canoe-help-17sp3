Bus Bars

 

In addition to making it possible to connect each channel to Vbatt or Gnd (battery voltage or ECU ground), the VT2516 Digital Module also provides a bus bar.

A relay can be used to lay each channel's signal line onto the internal bus bar. These can then be used to transmit the signal to an external device or another VT System module via the bus bar connections. Several channels can also be bundled internally via one bus bar.

The image illustrates the wiring of a bus bar on the VT2516.

The bus bars always have two external lines. In the case of the VT2516, the relevant reference potential is connected to the second line (bus bar line b). This is either ECU ground or battery voltage, depending on the use case.

Measurement devices generally expect the lower potential at bus bar pin b and the higher potential at pin a. The bus bar lines can thus be switched internally before they are laid on the bus bar connection.

These two examples serve to further clarify the use of bus bar relays:

Example 1

The door switch ECU pin is connected to the VT2516. A switch referenced to ground, which is connected to this ECU pin in the vehicle, indicates whether the door is closed or not. The bus bar is now used to measure the voltage across this switch. The VT2516 can of course also execute this measurement, but we are assuming here that an especially precise level of measurement is needed and that an external device is used for this purpose.

  • The measurement device is connected to the bus bar (pin a = plus, pin b = minus).
  • The signal is laid on the bus bar using the relevant channel's bus bar relay.
  • The ground reference potential is connected to bus bar pin b.

Example 2

The control LED ECU pin is connected to the VT2516. A small control LED is connected to the battery voltage at this ECU pin in the vehicle. The ECU has a switch referenced to mass, which closes to light the LED (low side switch).

  • The measurement device is connected to the bus bar (pin a = plus, pin b = minus).
  • The signal is laid on the bus bar using the relevant channel's bus bar relay.
  • The battery voltage reference potential is connected to bus bar pin b.
  • The bus bar polarity is reversed so that the higher potential is connected externally to pin a.
    The measurement device would otherwise show a negative voltage.

Note: The measurement device can also be connected with polarity reversed (pin a = minus, pin b = plus); this would also correct the displayed polarity. However this solution is not generally recommended, since you may also want to use the measurement device on the bus bar for other channels.

Connecting the Bus Bars to other VT Modules

If there are no special system requirements, we recommend connecting the bus bars of all VT modules in the following way:

  • Connect the battery voltage and ground (Vbatt and Gnd) to all modules.
    This lets you short all lines to Vbatt and ground, and makes it possible to simulate switches referenced to Vbatt or ground.
    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004 AND VT2004, connect Vbatt and ground to bus bar 1 (pin a = Vbatt, pin b = Gnd).
    Other modules, such as the VT2516, have dedicated connections for Ubat and ground, which replace bus bar 1.
  • A bus bar can connect all modules, regardless of module type.
    This lets you generate short circuits between pins on different modules.
    You can also connect special measurement devices to this bus bar.
    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, bus bar 2 is used for this purpose.
    In the case of modules that have one bus bar, such as the VT2516, the sole bus bar is used.

The bus bar connections as well as the Vbatt/ground connections (if present) on all modules are arranged so that the respective pins can be connected directly (bus with 4 lines).

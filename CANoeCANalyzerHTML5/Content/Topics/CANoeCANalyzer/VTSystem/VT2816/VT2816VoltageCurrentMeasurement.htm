Voltage and Current Measurement

 

The VT2816 measures voltages and currents, pre-processes the results and makes them available in CANoe via a set of corresponding system variables.

The measured momentary values are used to derive average and RMS values across various integration intervals (see system variables). The maximum and minimum values are also derived from the momentary values.

To achieve a better resolution, you can switch the voltage measurement range from the default ±60 V to ±10 V. You can measure the voltage differential between the input and output of a given channel, as well as relative to ground. To measure relative to ground, you connect line b of that channel to AGND. When you do this, make sure to connect the measurement ground potential (AGND) to the ground potential of the VT System (DGND).
For a more detailed explanation see VT System Ground Connection.

Currents are measured indirectly, by measuring the voltage drop across a special, very low-ohm measurement resistor (shunt). Channels 1-8 each have an additional current measurement input, which you can switch to when you want to measure currents with a given channel.

You can use the following measurement modes:

  • Voltage difference between lines a and b; measurement range ±60 V (default mode).
  • Voltage difference between lines a and b; measurement range ±10 V.
  • Voltage on line a to AGND; measurement range ±60 V.
  • Voltage on line a to AGND; measurement range ±10 V.
  • Current measurement within a range of ±5 A (channels 1-8 only)

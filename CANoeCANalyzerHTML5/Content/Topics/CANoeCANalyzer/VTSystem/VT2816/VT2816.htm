VT2816 – General Purpose Analog I/O Module

 

General

The VT2816 General-Purpose Analog I/O Module has 12 channels for measuring analog voltages, of which 8 can also be used to measure currents. You can output analog voltages on 4 further independent channels.

The measurement channels have a differential layout, but can also operate as single-ended channels if needed. In this case the reference potential is AGND. The output channels can also operate as differential or single-ended channels. The reference potential here is either DGND or the ECU ground, which is connected externally.

You can switch the voltage measurement range between ±60 V and ±10 V for more precise measurement.
The current measurement range is ±5 A.
You can switch the output voltage range between ±10 V and 0…28 V. The outputs can carry up to 200 mA.

Switching Different Signal Paths

Usage, Functions, Technical Data

The VT2816 has two different measurement channels. You can measure both voltage and current on channels 1-8. This is done using different inputs, but further processing is handled by the same electronics with relays that are switched at the input.

Channels 9-12 are wired exclusively for voltage measurement, and don't offer any switching at the input.

You can output analog voltages on channels 13-16.

The VT2816 does not have a bus bar for external voltages. However, you can switch line b of an output channel to DGND or to a common potential that is applied externally.

Notice!

  • Correct grounding is absolutely essential when operating VT modules.

    In particular, you need to connect AGND with DGND.

    You also need to make sure to connect the ECU ground to the VT2816 when the output channels are not operated in differential mode.

    If these connections are missing, the VT System module may be damaged!

  • For more information, see VT System Ground Connection.

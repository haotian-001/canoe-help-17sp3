Bus Bars

 

The VT1004/VT1104 has two bus bars on which the lines a and b of the four channels can be switched. The two lines and can be switched independently of one another, i.e., channel line a can be switched independently of channel line b to line a in respect to line b of one of the two bus bars.

The module also has swap relays between the bus bar connection and the internal bus bar lines on both bus bars. These relays can be switched individually, so that for instance bus bar input a can be switched onto both internal bus bar lines a and b.

Via the bus bar connections a signal switched to the bus bar can be transmitted to an external device, additionally several channels can be bundled via one bus bar.

Bus bar 1 typically carries Vbatt and Gnd (battery voltage and device ground), so that Vbatt and Gnd based short circuits of individual channel lines can be generated via the corresponding bus bar relays. However, this means that line a can initially only be connected to Vbatt and line b to Gnd.

By swapping the bus bar lines (i.e. both swap relays for bus 1 are on), you can also create ground based short circuits on channel line a and Vbatt based short circuits on channel line b. Bus bar line swapping is a module property, not a property of the individual channels. For this reason, you need to adjust the corresponding settings in the module namespace, not in the channel namespace.

Bus bar 2 remains freely available and can be used for customization. For instance, it can be used to connect an external load in case the internal electronic load is insufficient. It can also be used to generate short circuits between the lines connecting different channels.

Connecting the Bus Bars to other VT Modules

If there are no special system requirements, we recommend connecting the bus bars of all VT modules in the following way:

  • Connect the battery voltage and ground (Vbatt and Gnd) to all modules.
    This lets you short all lines to Vbatt and ground, and makes it possible to simulate switches referenced to Vbatt or ground.
    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004/VT1104 and VT2004, connect Vbatt and ground to bus bar 1 (pin a = Vbatt, pin b = Gnd).
    Other modules, such as the VT2516, have dedicated connections for Vbatt and ground, which replace bus bar 1.
  • A bus bar can connect all modules, regardless of module type.

    This lets you generate short circuits between pins on different modules.

    You can also connect special measurement devices to this bus bar.

    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004/VT1104 and VT2004, bus bar 2 is used for this purpose.

    In the case of modules that have one bus bar, such as the VT2516, the sole bus bar is used.

The bus bar connections as well as the Vbatt/Gnd connections (if present) on all modules are arranged so that the respective pins can be connected directly (bus with 4 lines).

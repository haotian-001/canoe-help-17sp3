Bus Bars

 

The VT6104/6204 network module has an internal bus bar. You can use a relay to allocate the High signal line of each channel to line A of the internal bus bar, and the Low signal line to line B of the internal bus bar. This makes it possible to connect the bus lines internally and to switch High and Low to other potentials or connect them to an external device (e.g. an oscilloscope). Additionally the module's bus bars have a swap relay between the bus bar connector and the internal bus bars lines. Via this relay the two input lines a and b of the bus bar can be switched directly or swapped to the lines a and b of the internal bus bar. Since a common (shared) relay is used, always both system variables have to be activated for swapping the two lines.

Connecting the Bus Bars to other VT Modules

If there are no special system requirements, we recommend connecting the bus bars of all VT modules in the following way:

  • Connect the battery voltage and ground (Vbatt and Gnd) to all modules.
    This lets you short all lines to Vbatt and ground, and makes it possible to simulate switches referenced to Vbatt or ground. Some modules like the VT6104/6204 have dedicated connections for Vbatt/ground. In other modules, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, these connections are included in the first bus bar. In the case of modules with two bus bars, Vbatt/ground is connected to bus bar 1.
  • A bus bar can connect all modules, regardless of module type.
    This lets you generate short circuits between pins on different modules. You can also connect special measurement devices to this bus bar. In the case of modules with two bus bars, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, you use bus bar 2 for this purpose. In the case of modules that have one bus bar, such as the VT6104/6204 and VT2516, you use the single bus bar.

The bus bar connections as well as the Vbatt/ground connections (if present) on all modules are arranged so that the respective pins can be connected directly (bus with 4 lines).

Notice!

The dedicated Vbatt/ground connections of the VT6104/VT6204 and VT2516 always need to be connected, even if they are not needed e.g. for short circuits.

You should always connect ground when the module is in operation (i.e. when it is powered). If you don't, you could damage the module.

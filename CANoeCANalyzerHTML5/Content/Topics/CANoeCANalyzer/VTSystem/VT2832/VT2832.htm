VT2832 – Switch Matrix Module

 

General

The Switch Matrix Module VT2832 allows arbitrary switching of 8 columns (channels) with 4 rows (8×4 switch matrix). Additional 4 paths can be switched directly. By using Solid State Relays it is possible to switch currents up to 16 A under full load at voltages up to 60 V on each channel.

The overall current per channel and the voltage of the path against DGND are measured on each channel.

The frequency and the duty cycle for periodic switching operations can be chosen individually for each channel.

Contact bouncing processes can be simulated by loading an arbitrarily defined bitstream on the module and executing it during the CANoe measurement.

Switching Different Signal Paths

Usage, Functions, Technical Data

Switching of signal paths is a core feature of the VT2832. The matrix setup allows arbitrary connections between Column 1 to Column 8 and Row 1 to Row 4 to be set. Multiple connections are also possible since the applied solid state relays are balancing the current more or less equally to all connected rails. Additionally, each of the switch lines Switch 1 to Switch 4 can be connected to one dedicated channel.

An overload protection is realized by monitoring of the total current on each channel. If the maximum continuous current of 16 A is exceeded the measurement will be terminated and all channels will be disconnected automatically. By type of construction, the maximum frequency of signals to be switched is limited to 100 kHz.

To switch higher currents, matrix channels can be bridged externally and switched by synchronous operation of the affected matrix nodes.

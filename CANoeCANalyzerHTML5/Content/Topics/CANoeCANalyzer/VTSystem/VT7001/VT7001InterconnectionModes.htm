Interconnection Modes

 

The three available power supply units (two external, one internal) and the two output line pairs (OUT1 with GND1 and OUT2 with GND2) can be connected to each other in a variety of ways. The VT7001/VT7101 power module thus allows for a variety of power supply configurations and use cases. You can even switch between these while the system is running.

The following interconnection modes are supported:

  • One Power Supply Only

    One of the three power supply units is used (modes: supint, sup1, sup2); it is connected to both outputs.

    This way you can power an ECU with two different power supply inputs (typically terminal 15 and terminal 30).
    The two power supply inputs can be switched and measured separately (current, voltage).

    Alternately you can also switch and control two ECUs, each with one power supply input, independently of each other.

  • Two Independent Power Supplies

    One of the three available power supply units is used for each output. Six combinations are possible here (modes: supint_sup1, supint_sup2, sup1_supint, sup1_sup2, sup2_supint, sup2_sup1).

    As with One Power Supply Only, you can power one ECU that has two separate power supply inputs (e.g. terminals 15 and 30) or two separate ECUs that each have one power supply input. In this configuration, the lines can supply different voltages, since you are using separate power supply units.

    You can also use this mode for the Battery voltage offset between two ECUs test case. You can measure voltage and current for the two ECUs separately.

  • Two Power Supplies in Series

    The two external power supply units can be connected serially. The two outputs, OUT1 and OUT2, are referenced to the same potential, but the two ground connections, GND1 and GND2, are not.

    This lets you connect two ECUs, with the ground lines of each referenced to different potentials. The difference is generated by power supply 2. You can use this mode to execute the Ground offset between two ECUs test case. You can measure voltage and current for the two ECUs separately.

    A serial connection can also be used for the Battery voltage offset test case. However, we recommend using the Two Independent Power Supplies mode for this scenario, because when you use two separate power supply units, the currents are measured separately and you can connect the two ECUs independently.

    A serial connection can also be used to supply voltage to one ECU. In this case you can use one of the two power supplies to superimpose a disturbance voltage.

  • Two Power Supplies in Parallel

    Two power supply units are connected in parallel. When you do so, there should be no or very little difference in voltage between the two. The VT7001/VT7101 measures both input voltages, and only permits the parallel connection if they differ by less than 1 V.

    Like the One Power Supply Only mode, you can use this interconnection mode to power either one or two ECUs. A parallel connection makes it possible to combine an electronic power supply unit with a buffer battery as the second power supply unit. This lets you execute tests in which the ECU being tested takes up very high currents in short bursts, which the first power supply unit alone is incapable of providing.

Example: Powering Clamps 15 and 30 Separately

In this example, the two external power supply units are connected to Power Supply 1 (namespace PS30) and Power Supply 2 (PS15). The ECU is connected to OUT1 (clamp 30, namespace Clamp30), OUT2 (clamp 15, Clamp15) and GND1 (clamp 31). The VT7001/VT7101 module is named PowerSupply.
The two ECU inputs can now be controlled independently via the two power supply units.

CAPL

// set mode
sysvar::VTS::PowerSupply.SetInterconnectionMode (eVTSInterconnectionModeSup1Sup2);

// set voltage mode for both external power supplies
// factor is set to 0.1: 1 V control voltage -> 10 V output at power supply
sysvar::VTS::PS30.SetRefVoltageMode (eVTSRefVoltageModeConstant, 0.1);
sysvar::VTS::PS15.SetRefVoltageMode (eVTSRefVoltageModeConstant, 0.1);

// set output voltage of clamp30 to 13.5 V, activate output
@sysvar::VTS::PS30::RefVoltage = 13.5;
@sysvar::VTS::Clamp30::Active = eVTSOutputModeActive;

TestWaitForTimeout (500);  // wait 0.5 s

// set output voltage of clamp15 to 15 V, activate output
@sysvar::VTS::PS15::RefVoltage = 15.0;
@sysvar::VTS::Clamp15::Active = eVTSOutputModeActive;

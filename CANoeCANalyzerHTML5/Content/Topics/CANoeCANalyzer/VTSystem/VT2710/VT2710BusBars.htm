Bus Bars

 

The VT2710 is equipped with two bus bars with the lines a and b. The two lines can be switched, independently of one other, to the signal and supply lines of the serial automotive interfaces PSI5 or SENT.

The module also offers swap relays between the bus bar connection and the internal bus bar lines on both bus bars. These relays can be switched individually, so that for instance bus bar input a can be switched onto both internal bus bar lines a and b.

Via the bus bar connections a signal switched to the bus bar can be transmitted to an external device, additionally several channels can be bundled via one bus bar.

By default, the Vbatt and GND (battery voltage and device ground) are connected to the bus bar 1 so that it is possible to generate short-circuits from the individual signal lines to Vbatt and GND. The bus bar 2 is freely available.

The signals of the serial automotive interfaces PSI5 and SENT can be freely switched to this bus bar. Therefore, any connection configuration is possible.

Note

The maximum VDD/Sensor voltage for VT2710 SENT channels are +6 V. By using the short circuit option via the bus bar 1 to Vbatt, the Vbatt voltage shall be < +6 V.

Connecting the Bus Bars to other VT Modules

If there are no special system requirements, we recommend connecting the bus bars of all VT modules in the following way:

  • Connect the battery voltage and ground (Vbatt and Gnd) to all modules.
    This lets you short all lines to Vbatt and ground, and makes it possible to simulate switches referenced to Vbatt or ground.
    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004 AND VT2004, connect Vbatt and ground to bus bar 1 (pin a = Vbatt, pin b = Gnd).
    Other modules, such as the VT2516, have dedicated connections for Ubat and ground, which replace bus bar 1.
  • A bus bar can connect all modules, regardless of module type.
    This lets you generate short circuits between pins on different modules.
    You can also connect special measurement devices to this bus bar.
    In the case of modules that have two bus bars, such as the VT1004 and VT2004, bus bar 2 is used for this purpose.
    In the case of modules that have one bus bar, such as the VT2516, the sole bus bar is used.

The bus bar connections as well as the Vbatt/Gnd connections (if present) on all modules are arranged so that the respective pins can be connected directly (bus with 4 lines).

Basics

 

ARINC word format

In ARINC 429, data are transmitted in an ARINC word with the following format:

The ARINC word contains fields with a defined meaning:

  • P: Parity bit

    Definition: Parity bit

    Bit 31 contains the parity bit for the ARINC word. The specification sets the parity to “odd”.
  • SSM: Sign/Status Matrix
  • data: Area with application data
  • SDI: Source/Destination Identifier

  • label: ARINC label

    Definition: ARINC label

    Each ARINC word is identified uniquely on the bus by an ARINC label. The label can assume values of 1..255 and is specified as an octal value in the specification (see /1/). The transmission on the bus takes place as bit-inverted data.

    If an octal representation is carried out within CANoe, a small “o” is appended to the octal value.

Transmission on the bus

There is one sender in each case on an ARINC 429 bus. All other network nodes connected to the bus are receivers. ARINC words are transmitted at an agreed communication speed, and the electrical signal on the bus cables is RZ-coded.

Transmission can take place at low-speed (12..14.5 kbit/s) or high-speed (100 kbit/s). The permissible tolerances of the transmission rate are shown in the following table. The average transmission speed can deviate by up to 1% over the duration of the ARINC word. For an individual bit, a deviation of 2.5% is allowed.

The sender determines the order and the interval of the individual messages. The specification /1/ stipulates that the interval between two consecutive ARINC words (gap) must be at least 4 bit times. This gap must also be maintained during cyclic communication.

Calculation of busload

Calculation principles:

  • The busload is counted for a valid message plus 4 bits of fixed gap.
  • When a message (Tx) is interrupted, the busload is counted up to the interruption plus 4 bits of gap (fixed value).
  • When a message (Rx) is interrupted, the busload is counted up to the interruption plus 4 bits of gap (fixed value). If a ‘1’ or ‘0’ (10 V or -10 V) is detected on the bus during the gap phase, the gap is restarted.
  • If the gap and the following message overlap, this is not double counted. The maximum of the busload is thus 100%.

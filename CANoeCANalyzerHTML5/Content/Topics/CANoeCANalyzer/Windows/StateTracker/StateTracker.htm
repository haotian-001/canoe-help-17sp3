State Tracker

Ribbon: Analysis Tab|State Tracker|View / New

General

You can use the State Tracker to analyze states, state transitions, signals and CAN messages in order to visualize temporal dependencies. The data can stem from a range of sources (e.g. XCP, buses, I/Os on VTs) that are transmitted via system variables, signals, diagnostic parameters or CAN messages.

The State Tracker combines the functionality of a logic analyzer with the look & feel of a Vector analysis window.

Use Cases and Requirements

Usage and Configuration

Typical Use Cases

  • Troubleshooting and monitoring a system by analyzing the chronological sequence of states, signals and state transitions.
  • Joint analysis of diverse data, such as:
  • Internal ECU communication states (variables, ports, BSW and SWC states)
  • Bus signals
  • ECU I/Os
  • Monitoring the states of AUTOSAR Runnables
  • Reading out runnable states via the Vector hardware
  • Simple analysis of binary signals and digital I/Os

Variables, objects (e.g. signals and messages), and networks are inserted via the context menu; they show up in the Legend once you've inserted them.

The display of the Name column considers the global setting of the Options dialog.

The window is divided into two areas: Navigation and Signal view.

The navigation offers two views: Legend and Search. In Legend view, you can configure signals and simple triggers. In Search view, you can search for signals by value.

When the defined conditions are met, the simple trigger triggers the pause or stop measurement actions during a measurement.

With markers you can highlight any measurement time, e.g. to measure the time between markers and jump to any marker.

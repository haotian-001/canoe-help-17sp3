Bit

Description

Early
Termination

1: Message transmission was aborted prematurely because

  • there is no recipient,
  • a CRC error was detected,
  • or the receive buffer is full.

0: Transmission was not aborted prematurely.

NAck

1: The recipient did not receive the message and acknowledged it negatively. In the case of group/broadcast addressing, at least one recipient did not receive the message and acknowledged it negatively. Negative acknowledgment means that either the receive buffer is full or a CRC error was detected (see bits 3 (RxBufFull) and 2 (CRC Error)).

0: Message receipt was not negatively acknowledged. This may be due to the following causes:

  • Node messages (Rx/Tx): Message was received. If this is the case, the Ack bit is set.
  • Spy messages: The spy may be positioned before the recipient in the ring.
  • Dir = TX: Messages whose recipient address is not present in the ring. In this case, the Ack bit is also not set.

Ack

1: The recipient received the message and acknowledged it positively. In the case of group/broadcast addressing, at least one recipient received the message and acknowledged itpositively.

0: Receipt of the message was not positively acknowledged. This may be due to the following causes:

  • Spy messages: The spy may be positioned before the recipient in the ring.
  • Dir = TX: Messages whose recipient address is not present in the ring. In this case, the NAck bit is also not set.

RxBufFull

1: Receive buffer is full (RxBufFull).

0: Receive buffer is available.

CRC Error

1: CRC transmission error (CRC Error).

0: No CRC transmission error.

Valid Receipt

1: Valid receipt. The recipient acknowledged the message positively. In the case of group/broadcast addressing, at least one recipient acknowledged the message positively.

0: Invalid receipt. The recipient did not send positive acknowledgment for the message. In this case, the Ack bit is also not set. This may be due to the following causes:

  • Spy messages: The spy may be positioned before the recipient in the ring.
  • Dir = TX: Messages whose recipient address is not present in the ring

No Response

1: No response. The recipient acknowledged the message neither positively nor negatively. In this case, neither the Ack nor the NAck bit is set. This may be due to the following causes:

  • Spy messages: The spy may be positioned before the recipient in the ring.
  • Dir = TX: Messages whose recipient address is not present in the ring

0: The recipient has acknowledged receipt of the message (positive or negative!). In the case of group/broadcast addressing, at least one recipient sent positive or negative acknowledgment for the message.
